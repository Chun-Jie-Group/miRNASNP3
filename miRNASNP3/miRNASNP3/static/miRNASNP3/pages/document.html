What's new in miRNASNP-v3 compared to the previous version?

As the number of SNPs included in the dbSNP has increased significantly, and many new miRNAs have been discovered in the past few years, we released miRNASNP-v3. In this new version, several new features are added, including:

  • phenotypes for variations located in pre-miRNA and gene 3'UTR,
  • analysis of target gain/loss caused by variations in miRNA seed and gene 3'UTR,
  • gene enrichment analysis of miRNA targets gain and loss.

Phenotype

In the phenotype-related variations module, we integrated pathological information of 175,985 mutations from COSMIC, disease information of 32,136 variations from ClinVar and disease/trait information of 235,148 SNPs from GWAS catalog. We divided these variations into three categories based on their location. For variation in miRNA seed regions and gene 3'UTRs, target gain/loss information was presented. For variations in pre-miRNAs, we provided the minimum free energy change of pre-miRNA second structure caused by the variation, users can click the pre-miRNA to look into pre-miRNA details. In this module, users can screen for variations associated with a particular phenotype by selecting a tissue of interest or typing in a description of the phenotype.

Enrichment analysis for gained/lost targets

Enrichment analysis of gained/lost targets by variations in miRNA seed region may help us further discover the underlying biological mechanisms effected by the variation. We perform Gene Ontology (GO) and Kyoto Encyclopedia of Genes and Genomes (KEGG) enrichment analysis on gained/lost gene sets with gene numbers from 50 to 1000.

miRNA information and gene 3'UTR

The host gene information of miRNAs was extracted from mirna_context.txt file. For miRNAs without host gene, we label them as intergenic. The gene 3'UTR file and human genome sequence(GRCh38) were downloaded by the UCSC Table browser, then we use bedtools to get the sequence information for each transcript based on the genome position. To get a more reliable transcript set, gene symbols and RefSeq mRNA ID corresponding to the transcript ID was downloaded from Ensembl, transcripts which not have gene symbol or RefSeq mRNA ID were excluded. Finally, 34,116 transcripts(transcripts with a sequence length greater than 6(seed region length)) of 18,286 genes were ultimately used for target analysis.

Expression level and expression correlation of miRNAs and target genes

Gene expression data and miRNA expression data across 33 cancers were downloaded from TCGA. For each cancer, We performed pearson correlation analysis between gene and miRNA. The overall correlation coefficient value was displayed in loss targets information panel, which can be clicked to pop out a histogram of correlation coefficient value for each cancer type. However, because there is no expression data for SNP type 3'UTR/miRNA, the correlation coefficient value for gain targets is not available.

Genome-wide association studies (GWAS)

GWAS tagSNPs and SNPs in linkage disequilibrium(LD) region of the tagSNP were downloaded from GWAS catalog website for 26 populations. Then, an integrated LD region for tagSNPs related to a specific SNP of each population is calculated by SNPs in tagSNP LD regions. (setting LD analysis regions ±250kb around SNP position)

Online prediction

miRNASNP-v3 provided 3 kinds of online predictions. Predicting the impact of variant in gene 3'UTR on miRNA:mRNA interaction will scan 2,652 mature miRNAs to predict target miRNAs for wild-type and SNP-type 3'UTR sequence, which provided by users, by miRmap and TargetScan. Then follow the analysis pipeline constructed in miRNASNP to get the gain/loss targets. Predicting the impact of variant in miRNA on miRNA:mRNA interaction will scan 18,286 gene 3'UTRs to predict target genes for wild-type and SNP-type miRNA sequence.(In order to reduce the calculation time, only the longest transcript of each gene was used to take online prediction in Tools module.) The prediction result will provide a summary panel, and another four panels to show gain targets, loss targets, wild-type and SNP-type sequence predicted targets. For predicting the impact of variations in pre-miRNA on miRNA structure, the minimum free energy(MFE) of SNP-type sequence for each SNP is predicted byRNAfold.

Target gain/loss

We provide the effect of SNPs/variations in miRNA seed regions. For SNPs in miRNA seed region, we got miRNA wild sequence and SNP allele sequence. Then, we used two target prediction tools TargetScan and miRmap to predict their target sites respectively. Four categories of results are recorded as wild TargetScan site (WT), wild miRmap site (WM), SNP TargetScan site (ST) and SNP miRmap site (SM). If one miRNA's target gene shows in both WT and WM, but not in either ST or SM, we called that the miRNA lost this target gene. On the contrary, if one target shows in both ST and SM, but not in either WT or WM, we called the miRNA gained one target gene.

We also provides the effect of SNPs in genes' 3'UTRs. For SNPs in one gene UTR, we got wild type 3'UTR sequence and corresponding SNP 3'UTR sequence. Then, we used two target prediction tools (TargetScan and miRmap) to predict their target sites respectively. Four categories of results are recorded as wild TargetScan site (WT), wild miRmap site (WM), SNP TargetScan site (ST), SNP miRmap site (SM). If one target site shows in both WT and WM, but not in either ST or SM, we called that the gene lost this target site. On the contrary, if one target both show in ST and SM, but not in either WT or WM, we called the gene gained one target site.
