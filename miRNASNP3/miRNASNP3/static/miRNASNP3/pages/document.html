miRNASNP-v3 document

Features in miRNASNP-v3

The completion of international projects (1000 genome project, TCGA, ExAC et al.) introduced a large amount of new single nucleotide polymorphism (SNPs). These SNPs in miRNA can impact the secondary structure and targets. miRNASNP-v3 characterized SNPs in miRNA and gene 3'UTR and predict the effect of miRNA:mRNA interaction and secondary structure. Also, miRNASNP-v3 identified disease related variations (DRVs).

Updated the previous database with latest data, including:

  • SNPs in pre-miRNAs and their impacts on miRNAs.
  • Targets gain/loss by SNPs in miRNA seed and gene 3'UTR.
  • Online tools to predict novel SNP effect on miRNA function.
  • Experiment validated miRNA:gene interaction.
  • Expression and correlation of miRNAs and genes.

New features are added to miRNASNP-v3, including:

  • Gene enrichment of miRNA targets gain and loss.
  • DRVs (GWAS, ClinVAR, COSMIC) in miRNA and 3'UTR.
  • DRVs impact miRNA target gain/loss and secondary structure.

Characterized variation in miRNAs and genes

Experimentally verified microRNA-target interactions (MTIs)

We integrated experimentally verified microRNA-target interactions (MTIs) from the databases of starBase, TarBase, miRTarBase, miRecords and miR2Disease. We downloaded 370,058 MTIs with supporting CLIP-seq (HITS-CLIP, PAR-CLIP, iCLIP, CLASH) experiments (>5) from starBase. 422,390 and 37,490 MTIs were collected from TarBase and miRTarBase respectively, they are validated by report assay, western blot and high-throughput experiment. We also integrated 240 MTIs from miRecords experimentally verified by literature curation, and 641 MTIs from miR2Disease by Luciferase reporter assay. Generally, 1027355 experimentally verified MTIs were included in miRNASNP-v3. It should be noted that, miRNA names were uniformed by miRBase nomenclature, while gene name was used the gene symbol.

miRNA expression and correlation with mRNA

Gene expression data and miRNA expression data across 33 cancers were downloaded from TCGA. For each cancer, We performed pearson correlation analysis between gene and miRNA. The overall correlation coefficient value was displayed in loss targets information panel, which can be clicked to pop out a histogram of correlation coefficient value for each cancer type. However, because there is no expression data for SNP type 3'UTR/miRNA, the correlation coefficient value for gain targets is not available.

Disease related variations in miRNA/3'UTR

In the disease-related variations module, we integrated pathological information of 175,985 mutations from COSMIC, disease information of 32,136 variations from ClinVar and disease/trait information of 235,148 SNPs from NHGRI GWAS catalog. We divided these variations into three categories based on their location. For variation in miRNA seed regions and gene 3'UTRs, target gain/loss information was presented. For variations in pre-miRNAs, we provided the minimum free energy change of pre-miRNA secondary structure caused by the variation, users can click the pre-miRNA to look into pre-miRNA details. In this module, users can screen for variations associated with a particular disease by selecting a tissue of interest or typing in a description of the disease.

To look into GWAS SNPs, we downloaded all the tagSNPs from NHGRI GWAS catalog with the LD information between the tagSNP and the surrounding SNPs (D'>0.98, a LD mesurement to show the degree of linkage disequilibrium between the selected variant and other variants) of 26 populations. Then, a genome block between the minimum and maximum coordination of the sorrounded SNPs was defined as the GWAS identified trait associated regions of each tagSNP in each population. Finally, we identified 3,602 tagSNPs and 236,251 SNPs in LD region, and for each SNP, a plot of the GWAS identified trait associated regions was presented (Plot regionsâ€‰±250kb around SNP position).

Enrichment analysis for gained/lost targets

Enrichment analysis of gained/lost targets by variations in miRNA seed region may help us further discover the underlying biological mechanisms effected by the variation. We perform Gene Ontology (GO) and Kyoto Encyclopedia of Genes and Genomes (KEGG) enrichment analysis on gained/lost gene sets with gene numbers from 50 to 1000.

Online prediction

miRNASNP-v3 provided 3 kinds of online predictions. Predicting the impact of variant in gene 3'UTR on miRNA:mRNA interaction will scan 2,652 mature miRNAs to predict target miRNAs for wild-type and SNP-type 3'UTR sequence, which provided by users, by miRmap and TargetScan. Then follow the analysis pipeline constructed in miRNASNP to get the gain/loss targets. Predicting the impact of variant in miRNA on miRNA:mRNA interaction will scan 18,286 gene 3'UTRs to predict target genes for wild-type and SNP-type miRNA sequence.(In order to reduce the calculation time, only the longest transcript of each gene was used to take online prediction in Tools module.) The prediction result will provide a summary panel, and another four panels to show gain targets, loss targets, wild-type and SNP-type sequence predicted targets. For predicting the impact of variations in pre-miRNA on miRNA structure, the minimum free energy(MFE) of SNP-type sequence for each SNP is predicted byRNAfold.

Target gain/loss

We provide the effect of SNPs/variations in miRNA seed regions. For SNPs in miRNA seed region, we got miRNA wild sequence and SNP allele sequence. Then, we used two target prediction tools TargetScan and miRmap to predict their target sites respectively. Four categories of results are recorded as wild TargetScan site (WT), wild miRmap site (WM), SNP TargetScan site (ST) and SNP miRmap site (SM). If one miRNA's target gene shows in both WT and WM, but not in either ST or SM, we called that the miRNA lost this target gene. On the contrary, if one target shows in both ST and SM, but not in either WT or WM, we called the miRNA gained one target gene.

We also provides the effect of SNPs in genes' 3'UTRs. For SNPs in one gene UTR, we got wild type 3'UTR sequence and corresponding SNP 3'UTR sequence. Then, we used two target prediction tools (TargetScan and miRmap) to predict their target sites respectively. Four categories of results are recorded as wild TargetScan site (WT), wild miRmap site (WM), SNP TargetScan site (ST), SNP miRmap site (SM). If one target site shows in both WT and WM, but not in either ST or SM, we called that the gene lost this target site. On the contrary, if one target both show in ST and SM, but not in either WT or WM, we called the gene gained one target site.
