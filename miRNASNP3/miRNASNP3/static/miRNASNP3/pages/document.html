miRNASNP-v3 document

Features in miRNASNP-v3

The completion of international projects (1000 genome project, TCGA, ExAC et al.) introduced a large amount of new single nucleotide polymorphism (SNPs). These SNPs in miRNA can impact the secondary structures and targets of miRNAs. miRNASNP-v3 characterized SNPs in miRNAs, gene 3'UTRs and could predict the effect of miRNA-target interactions (MTIs) and secondary structures. Also, miRNASNP-v3 identified disease related variations (DRVs).

Updated the previous database with latest data, including:

  • SNPs in pre-miRNAs and their impacts on miRNAs.
  • Target gain/loss by SNPs in miRNA seed region and gene 3'UTRs.
  • Online tools to predict novel SNP effect on miRNA function.
  • Experimentally verified miRNA-target interaction.
  • Expression and correlation of miRNAs and genes.

New features are added to miRNASNP-v3, including:

  • DRVs (NHGRI GWAS catalog, ClinVar, COSMIC) in miRNAs and gene 3'UTRs.
  • DRVs impact on miRNA target gain/loss and secondary structure.
  • Gene enrichment of miRNA target gain/loss by variations in miRNA seed regions.
  • Correlation of drug sensitivity and miRNAs.

Characterized variations in miRNAs and genes

The characterized variations in miRNASNP-v3 comprise single nucleotide polymorphism (SNP) in miRNAs/genes and disease related variation (DRV) in miRNAs/genes. More than 600 million SNPs, 21 million COSMIC non-coding variations and 444 thousands ClinVar variations were mapped to 2,652 mature miRNAs derived from 1,913 pre-miRNAs. We performed target gain/loss analysis for variations in miRNA seed region and 3'UTR. The seed region is defined as a 7bp nucleotide fragment at the 5'end of the mature miRNA between 2 and 8. Meanwhile, we studied the impact of all the variations in pre-miRNA on secondary structure. The detailed mapping information is listed below.

Target gain/loss

We provide the target gain/loss effect of SNPs in miRNA seed regions. Firstly, for SNPs in miRNA seed region, we obtained miRNA wild sequence and SNP allele sequence. Then, we used two target prediction tools TargetScan and miRmap to predict their target sites respectively. Four categories of results are recorded as wild TargetScan site (WT), wild miRmap site (WM), SNP TargetScan site (ST) and SNP miRmap site (SM). If one miRNA's target gene shows in both WT and WM, but not in either ST or SM, we called that the miRNA lost this target gene. On the contrary, if one target shows in both ST and SM, but not in either WT or WM, we called that the miRNA gained one target gene.

We also provide the target gain/loss effect of SNPs in genes' 3'UTRs. For SNPs in one gene 3'UTR, we obtained wild type 3'UTR sequence and corresponding SNP 3'UTR sequence. Then, we used two target prediction tools (TargetScan and miRmap) to predict their target sites respectively. Four categories of results are recorded as wild TargetScan site (WT), wild miRmap site (WM), SNP TargetScan site (ST), SNP miRmap site (SM). If one target site shows in both WT and WM, but not in either ST or SM, we called that the gene lost this target site. On the contrary, if one target both show in ST and SM, but not in either WT or WM, we called that the gene gained one target site.

(This method is also used to analyze the effect of variations (from COSMIC and ClinVar) in miRNA seed regions and 3'UTRs on target gain/loss )

Enrichment analysis for target gain/loss

Enrichment analysis of gained/lost targets by variations in miRNA seed region may help us further discover the underlying biological mechanisms effected by the variation. We perform Gene Ontology (GO) and Kyoto Encyclopedia of Genes and Genomes (KEGG) enrichment analysis on gained/lost gene sets with gene numbers from 50 to 1000.

Correlation of drug sensitivity and miRNAs

Drug sensitivity data (GI50) of 18,724 drug/compounds and expression data of 355 miRNAs in NCI-60 were download from CellMinerCDB. The drug/compounds were annotated by NSC ID with its corresponding PubChem SID. In addition, sensitivity data of 24 drugs measured by IC50 were downloaded from Cancer Cell Line Encyclopedia (CCLE) with expression data of 626 miRNAs. Then we performed Spearman correlation analysis between drug sensitivity and miRNA expression. The result is present in miRNA detail panel. And we only list miRNA-compound pairs with P-value<0.05 and FDR<0.05 in NCI-60, while for CCLE, all the correlation coefficient value between drugs and miRNAs was presented.

Experimentally verified miRNA-target interactions (MTIs)

We integrated experimentally verified miRNA-target interactions (MTIs) from the databases of starBase, TarBase, miRTarBase, miRecords and miR2Disease. We downloaded 370,058 MTIs with supporting CLIP-seq (HITS-CLIP, PAR-CLIP, iCLIP, CLASH) experiments (>5) from starBase. 422,390 and 37,490 MTIs were collected from TarBase and miRTarBase respectively, they had been validated by luciferase report assay, western blot and high-throughput experiments. We also integrated 240 MTIs from miRecords experimentally verified by literature curation, and 641 MTIs from miR2Disease by luciferase reporter assay. Generally, 1,027,355 experimentally verified MTIs were obtained in miRNASNP-v3. It should be noted that, miRNA names were uniformed by miRBase nomenclature, and gene names was annotated by gene symbols.

miRNA expression and correlation with mRNAs

Gene expression data and miRNA expression data across 33 cancers were downloaded from TCGA portal. For each cancer, we performed pearson correlation analysis between genes and miRNAs. The overall correlation coefficient value was displayed in loss targets information panel, which can be clicked to pop out a histogram of correlation coefficient value for each cancer type. However, because there is no expression data for SNP type 3'UTR/miRNA, the correlation coefficient value for gain target is not available.

Online prediction

miRNASNP-v3 provides three kinds of online predictions. (1) Predicting the impact of variant in gene 3'UTR on miRNA:mRNA interaction. First, this method will scan 2,652 mature miRNAs to predict target miRNAs for wild-type and SNP-type 3'UTR sequence by miRmap and TargetScan. Then follow the analysis pipeline constructed in miRNASNP to get the gain/loss targets. (2) Predicting the impact of variant in miRNA on miRNA-target interaction, this method will scan 18,286 gene 3'UTRs to predict target genes for wild-type and SNP-type miRNA sequence.(In order to save the calculation time, only the longest transcript of each gene was used to take online prediction in this module.) The prediction result will give a summary panel and other four panels to show gain targets, loss targets, predicted targets of wild/SNP-type sequence. (3) For predicting the impact of variations in pre-miRNA on miRNA structure, the minimum free energy (MFE) of SNP-type sequence for each SNP is predicted by RNAfold.
